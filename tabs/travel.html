TRAVELLING TO CAVTAT AND DUBROVNIK
Cavtat is located at the very south of the Republic of Croatia - 15 km from Dubrovnik, and only 6 km from Dubrovnik International Airport.

Plan your trip

BY AIR
Traveling by plane is a convenient and comfortable way to visit Dubrovnik. Many European companies have regular flights to Dubrovnik or Zagreb. The Croatian national carrier, Croatia Airlines, provides regular flights from Zagreb to Dubrovnik. Dubrovnik airport "Cilipi" is located about 18 km from Dubrovnik's city center but only 6 km from Cavtat.

There are also many charter flights that are usually cheaper than regular flights.

International Flights from/to Zagreb:
Amsterdam, Barcelona, Berlin, Brussels, Copenhagen, Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, Gothenburg, Istanbul, London, Madrid, Moscow, Munich, Paris, Prague, Rome, Sarajevo, Skopje, Stockholm, Stuttgart, Vienna, Warsaw and Zurich.

BY SEA
Another pleasant way of traveling to Dubrovnik is by sea. There are international and coastal ferry lines arriving and departing from Dubrovnik. Jadrolinija is the main Croatian passenger ferry company, with the highest number of regular international and domestic lines. The other Croatian shipping and freight agency is Jadroagent Ltd.

BY RAIL
The rail network connects all major Croatian cities, except Dubrovnik. If you plan to travel by rail the best way is to arrive to Zagreb or Split and take the bus from there to Dubrovnik. There are direct trains to Slovenia, Hungary, Italy, Austria, Switzerland, Germany, Bosnia-Herzegovina and other countries. Indirect connections also exist, connecting Croatia to almost all other European countries.

BY CAR
Croatia has recently built many modern and comfortable motorways. For the last 150 km between Split and Dubrovnik a road offering beautiful views leads along the coast but also requiring cautious driving. For more information on the Croatian roads see Hrvatske Autoceste

For the on-line traffic situation, support, etc. see Croatian Car Club (HAK)
BY BUS
The bus service offers cheaper alternative for travel across Croatia and to Dubrovnik. Throughout Dubrovnik region, even the smallest villages have some sort of bus connection. There are very comfortable express buses for longer distances. In almost every larger town, there is a bus station (Autobusna stanica or Autobusni kolodvor) where tickets are sold and timetables are clearly displayed.

In Dubrovnik the main bus terminal is located in Gruž Port where both local and international coaches arrive.
