CAVTAT
Cavtat is the most southern town in Croatia, and the most practical way to reach the town is by air. Ä†ilipi (Dubrovnik) airport is just three miles away and it is well connected. The City itself has a population of 1,500 inhabitants, and is a part of the Dubrovnik Riviera (twenty kilometers south of Dubrovnik, and 3 km of the main coastal highway).

The Cavtat area abounds with Mediterranean vegetation which covers the whole area. Also, the sea in the Cavtat area is unpolluted and the city has a very attractive mixture of old and new architecture. Originally it was a Greek settlement called Epidauros. Around 228 BC it was under the Romans and later became a Roman colony. The name Cavtat originates from Civets vet us, as the fugitives in the newly established Dubrovnik used to call their first habitation.
